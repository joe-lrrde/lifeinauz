Poetry

For as long as I can remember I have been writing.


Ironically I wrote not because I had an overflowing imagination but because I simply wanted to put words on paper.


So I would write the same stories over and over again. And the more I grew the less I was able to finish them.


I think this is why I turned into poetry. It's harder to start a poem than it is finishing it.


Even if you write 3 lines, you can call it a poem. There are no rules to poetry. Because there are as many different types of poetry as there are writers. Poets.


I don't like that poetry is this art seen as copmlicated and untouchable. Don't get me wrong, I strongly believe that writing good poetry is one of the hardest things to do. But at the end of the day it just takes practice.


And what's more important is that you don't need to be a poet to enjoy poetry. Or rather, you're a poet even if you don't think you are. So read poetry. Don't shut the door to it. And if you don't like a poem, change poets.


Because I guarantee you will find poetry to your taste.
